Hi, I’m Amy! I’m a digital designer.


I’m a story-chaser. After studying Film & Media Studies at UC Santa Barbara, I got my start in the entertainment industry at a talent management company. I had the opportunity clients in the likes of Willem Dafoe and Francis McDormand (yes, I have so many stories). I continued on to be a showrunner’s assistant on Netflix’s KALEIDOSCOPE.

During production on KALEIDOSCOPE, I was asked to design a presentation deck that broke down the show’s design approach, and I recognized that visual design and film design share many of the same components. For example, type variation has the unique ability to unify a user experience, just like contrasting characters on-screen deliver a cohesive theme. I continued practicing and creating graphics on Photoshop and then Figma; I loved the idea of learning a new form of expression.

After production wrapped, I decided to dedicate the next year to learn how to code and build my portfolio. I earned a certificate in Front-End Web Development with General Assembly as well as a certificate in UX Design from Google.

Fast forward to January 2023 and I’m still chasing stories: the story a user creates through product interaction. I am passionate about designing and building products that make each user feel like they are the only person in the audience, that this story was designed for them.
