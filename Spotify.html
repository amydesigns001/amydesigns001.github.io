SpotifyQueue

You've been invited to the DJ booth.

Introducing a brand new feature to create a live queue with your friends.

Upvote your favorite songs and get them bumped to the top.

Downvote the ones you don't like so much and knock them out.

Save your memories for later.

And repeat.

1 / beginnings

The idea for this Spotify feature emerged one Friday night when about seven other friends and I were all gathered around a 13” MacBook Pro aggressively trying to add our songs to a queue. I thought, “what are we doing?”. We all have our own individual phones, and we all have Spotify downloaded. There has to be a better way to share a playlist, or a queue.

[Spotify Queue walks into the room.]

Spotify Queue is fun and simple way to add to a queue with your friends and make fun of each other for questionable song choices.

2 / challenges

A challenge that I faced while designing this feature was appealing to the already-busy user. The inferred user in mind is an individual that uses the app on a day-to-day basis and that doesn’t necessarily want to devote any additional screen time into the app.

With this in mind, I was able to make the argument that the feature should use already haptic controls that are already established within the app. Spotify’s slide to add to queue feature is something that users would already be familiar with, so I let that design guide the design of the upvote/downvote feature of the queue. It’s an efficient design choice that doesn’t require any downtime to learn, or build.

3 / goals

A major goal of this feature was to “not break the vibe” of the app. A feature that didn’t match the voice of Spotify would only devalue the holistic experience of the app. I was even hesitant to incorporate the red in the downvote indicator, but ultimately decided that the color indication was necessary for the game-aspect of the feature.

4 / solutions

- swipe to vote

- arrow side icon to indicate song movement

- song vote history

- save queue option

5 / the game of it all

In addition to using song votes to determine the queue’s order, votes are utilized when creating the “wrapped” portion of the queue. When the queue has finished, or is marked complete by its creator, a summary of the queue will be delivered to contributors–similar to Spotify Wrapped.
